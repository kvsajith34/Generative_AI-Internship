## 00:46 - 01:50 — The Old Way

Let me be honest. For the first two years of my channel, I did everything manually. I would sit down with a 45-minute video, scrub through the timeline, try to guess which clips would perform well, write captions from scratch, and then spend another three hours editing.

It was exhausting. Some weeks I posted three reels. Some weeks I posted zero. There was no system.

And the problem with doing everything manually is that you start to rely on intuition instead of data. You think a clip is funny or insightful, but your audience might not care.

## 01:51 - 03:00 — The Breaking Point

The breaking point came when I spent six hours editing a reel that got 400 views. Six hours. 400 views.

I realized I needed a repeatable workflow. Not just faster editing, but better decision-making. I needed to know which moments in my videos had the highest viral potential before I ever opened an editor.

That is when I started building a simple AI-powered pipeline around my content.

---

## 03:01 - 04:10 — Step 1: Transcribe Everything

The first step in my workflow is transcription. I take the long-form video, extract the audio, and run it through Whisper.

Why? Because once you have a timestamped transcript, you can analyze your content like a dataset. You can search for emotional peaks, clear lessons, surprising statements, and strong hooks.

Transcription turns your spoken content into something algorithms can work with. It is the foundation of everything that comes next.

---

## 04:11 - 05:05 — Step 2: Find the Viral Moments

Once I have the transcript, I score every segment using a few simple rules. I look for:

- Strong emotional language
- Clear before-and-after transformations
- Mistakes or lessons learned
- Surprising results or counterintuitive advice
- Phrases that sound like a quote

The highest-scoring segments become my reels. This removes guesswork. Instead of hoping a clip works, I am choosing clips that already contain viral ingredients.

---

## 05:06 - 06:15 — Step 3: Generate Hooks and Headlines

A great clip is useless without a great hook. So for each selected segment, I generate three different hook lines.

For example, if the segment is about saving time with automation, a hook might be: "I used to spend six hours on one reel. Now I make 30 in two hours. Here is the workflow."

That hook works because it has contrast, a number, and curiosity. Those are the same ingredients you see in viral reels every day.

---

## 06:16 - 07:20 — Step 4: Add Captions and Hashtags

Next, I generate a caption and a set of hashtags for each reel. The caption expands on the hook without giving everything away. The hashtags help the algorithm categorize the content.

I keep my hashtag mix simple: one broad niche tag, one platform-specific tag, one trending-style tag, and one creator-specific tag.

You do not need 30 hashtags. You need the right ones.

---

## 07:21 - 08:25 — Step 5: Plan B-roll with AI

Now here is the secret that makes the reels look professional: AI-planned B-roll.

For every segment, I write a short visual description of what should appear on screen while I am talking. Then I turn those descriptions into prompts for Runway.

For example: "A clean desk setup with two monitors showing a video editing timeline and an AI transcription panel. Soft morning light. Modern minimal aesthetic."

That prompt becomes a 5-second B-roll clip I can drop behind my talking head footage.

## 08:26 - 09:15 — The Biggest Mistake Beginners Make

The biggest mistake I see new creators make is trying to make every reel perfect. They spend two hours on one 15-second clip and burn out.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is a repeatable system. Publish, learn, improve.

A good reel posted this week beats a perfect reel that never gets posted.
